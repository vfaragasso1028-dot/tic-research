▸ Methodology & Signal Guide
📊 TicScore — CAN SLIM Scoring System (0–100 pts)

TicScore is a quantitative screen derived from William O'Neil's CAN SLIM methodology — designed to identify institutional-quality growth stocks before they make major price moves. Each component measures a distinct dimension of stock quality. Pass threshold is ≥50 pts. Scores ≥70 indicate a high-conviction setup.

Exempt sectors (Financials, Real Estate, Energy, Mining, Utilities, Clinical Biotech) are excluded from scoring because their earnings cycles don't conform to the growth-stock model.

G — Growth (0–40 pts)

The most heavily weighted component. Measures quarterly EPS growth (current quarter vs same quarter last year), annual EPS trend, and revenue acceleration.

CAN SLIM requires ≥25% quarterly EPS growth. Earnings acceleration — where each quarter beats the previous — is the strongest signal. A stock reporting 50%+ EPS growth with revenue confirmation is a Tier 1 setup.

V — Supply & Demand (0–20 pts)

Measures the float (tradeable shares) and recent relative volume. Smaller floats move more aggressively on institutional buying because supply is limited.

Relative volume above 1.5x average during a price breakout confirms institutional participation. High-volume up days with low-volume pullbacks is the ideal accumulation pattern.

Q — Quality (0–20 pts)

Return on Equity (ROE) ≥17% and profit margin expansion. ROE measures how efficiently management deploys capital — consistently high ROE is a moat indicator.

O'Neil specifically required ≥17% ROE as a minimum threshold. Stocks with expanding margins (gross, operating, net) in consecutive quarters signal pricing power.

S — Sponsorship (0–20 pts)

Institutional ownership percentage and analyst consensus. The "N" in CAN SLIM (New) — new institutional sponsors buying the stock create sustained demand.

Look for increasing institutional count quarter-over-quarter, not just high ownership. A stock going from 200 to 350 institutional holders in 2 quarters is accumulation in progress.

Score Interpretation
70–100 ptsHigh conviction. Institutional-grade setup.
50–69 ptsConditional pass. Check chart structure.
0–49 ptsFail. Fundamentals don't support swing long.
📈 TicStage — Stan Weinstein Stage Analysis

Every stock cycles through four stages. Weinstein's framework (from Secrets for Profiting in Bull and Bear Markets) uses the 30-week moving average as the key dividing line. TIC Research uses the 150-day SMA as the direct proxy. The stage determines whether you should be long, flat, or short — regardless of fundamentals.

1
Basing
Price consolidating below or near a flattening 150 SMA. Volume drying up. Institutions accumulating quietly. Can last months.
Wait for breakout
2
Advancing
Price above a rising 150 SMA. Higher highs, higher lows. This is the only stage where swing longs have structural tailwind.
Optimal long zone
3
Topping
Price stalling near a flattening 150 SMA. Volume patterns diverging. Distribution — institutions selling into strength.
Reduce / avoid
4
Declining
Price below a declining 150 SMA. Lower lows, lower highs. No matter how cheap it looks, the trend is against you.
No long setups

PowerStack Override: Stage 2 is confirmed early when 9EMA > 21EMA > 50SMA all aligned above the 150 SMA. This stacking tells you the short, medium, and long-term trends are all pointing the same direction — the highest-probability continuation environment.

⚙️ Technical Indicators — What Each Signal Means
RSI (14) — Relative Strength Index

Measures momentum on a 0–100 scale. Calculated as average gain / average loss over 14 periods. Tells you if the stock is extended or has room to run.

70–100Overbought — mean reversion risk
40–70Neutral — trending zone
0–30Oversold — potential bounce zone

In a Stage 2 uptrend, RSI holding above 50 on pullbacks confirms trend strength. RSI dipping to 40–50 then bouncing = ideal swing entry.

MACD (12/26/9) — Trend Momentum

Plots the difference between 12-day and 26-day EMAs. The Signal line (9-day EMA of MACD) and Histogram show momentum direction and acceleration.

Bullish: MACD line above Signal · Histogram expanding positive · Both above zero = trend acceleration.

Bearish: MACD crosses below Signal · Histogram shrinking or negative · Divergence between price and MACD = distribution warning.

ADX (14) — Trend Strength

ADX measures how strong the current trend is, regardless of direction. DI+ measures upward directional pressure; DI− measures downward pressure.

ADX ≥ 40Very strong trend
ADX 25–40Strong trend in progress
ADX < 20Trendless / choppy

DI+ crossing above DI− with ADX rising = trend initiation signal. ADX ≥ 25 with DI+ > DI− = confirmed bull trend.

ATR (14) — Average True Range

Measures average daily price range over 14 periods. The fundamental tool for position sizing and stop placement.

Stop placement: Initial stops are typically set at 1.5–2.0x ATR below the entry. This keeps you outside normal noise while limiting catastrophic loss.

Position sizing: Risk $ per trade ÷ (ATR × 1.5) = share size. Never risk more than 1–2% of portfolio on a single ATR stop.

RS Rating — Relative Strength

Ranks price performance relative to the 52-week range (0–99 scale). This is not the same as RSI — it measures where the stock sits in its annual range.

80–99Sector leader — buy zone
60–79Neutral — monitor
0–59Laggard — avoid longs

IBD research shows the best breakout stocks have RS ≥ 85 before the breakout. Low RS on a breakout = likely false move.

Moving Average Crosses

Golden Cross: 50 SMA rises above 200 SMA. Signals that medium-term momentum has overtaken the long-term trend. Institutional algorithms trigger long signals on this event.

Death Cross: 50 SMA falls below 200 SMA. The macro structure is bearish. Even good news can fail to hold gains in a death cross environment. Wait for recapture before going long.

🔍 Pattern Recognition — What the System Detects
Pocket Pivot

Developed by Chris Kacher & Gil Morales. A Pocket Pivot occurs when today's volume on an up-day exceeds the highest volume of any down-day in the prior 10 sessions.

This signals institutional accumulation happening quietly — before a formal breakout. Pocket Pivots near the 10-day or 50-day MA are the highest-quality entries. Often precedes a 10–20% move.

Lower High Pattern

Three consecutive swing highs where each peak is lower than the last. Classic distribution structure — sellers are more aggressive than buyers at each rally attempt.

When a Lower High pattern coincides with declining volume on bounces and ADX flattening, it's a warning that the uptrend is exhausted. The correct play is to tighten stops or reduce exposure.

Gap Support Zones

An upward price gap (today's low > yesterday's high by 0.5%+) creates a zone of air between prior resistance and new support. These gaps often act as magnets when price pulls back.

Gap zones are structural support floors — institutions who bought the gap rarely let price fall back through it without defending. A gap fill that holds is a high-probability long re-entry.

Support & Resistance Levels

Computed from recent swing highs (resistance) and swing lows (support) using a 5-bar lookback. These are the price levels where supply and demand have historically reversed.

A close above resistance on above-average volume = breakout confirmation. A close below support with volume = breakdown. Price that oscillates at a level for 3+ sessions before breaking tends to see a stronger follow-through move.

Sector Rotation Logic

Capital doesn't disappear — it rotates between sectors based on the economic cycle. When Technology lags, Industrials or Energy may be leading. The Rotation section shows you which names within the sector have the most institutional momentum right now.

Trade toward the leaders, away from the laggers. If the subject ticker ranks in the bottom half of its sector, consider whether a higher-RS peer offers a better risk/reward entry in the same thesis.

News Confirmation Logic

Headlines are scored bullish/bearish/neutral via keyword pattern matching across two independent sources (Yahoo Finance, Finviz). The aggregate signal confirms or contradicts what the chart is saying.

Confirming: ≥60% bullish headlines. Contradicting: ≥60% bearish. Mixed: Split sentiment. Always verify headlines manually — the system flags direction, not magnitude.
